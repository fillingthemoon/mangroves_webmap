Pasir Ris Mangroves

General

Located just a 10 minute walk from Pasir Ris MRT, Pasir Ris Park is home to a carefully preserved six-hectare mangrove forest arranged along various trails and boardwalk networks located on the south-eastern side of the park1. This mangrove forest also attracts bird enthusiasts from all over Singapore to observe the various birds in their natural environment1. Compared to mangroves in other parts of Singapore, such as those in Sungei Buloh, the Pasir Ris mangroves have a lower biomass carbon density as they are collectively smaller, thinner, and more heavily distrubed2. Furthermore, much of the mangroves here are medium age due to land reclamantion in the late 1970s and mangrove replanting efforts in the late 1980s2. Currently, Pasir Park is still carrying out tree planting works in its mangrove forest, warmly inviting volunteers from all over to take part in the #OneMillionTreesSG movement3.
